# GrandMarket Retail Sales Analysis

## Overview

An in-depth analysis conducted for the Head of Operations to evaluate GrandMarket's performance over the past year. These insights will be utilized by a cross-functional team to increase GrandMarket's market performance. 

### Northstar Metrics

- **Sales Trends** - revenue, orders, and average order value (AOV)
-  **Product Performance** - Analyze different product categories and product impact
- **Loyalty Program** - Effectiveness evaluation and recommendations for customer retention

## Deep Dive Analysis
### 1. Sales Trends
![images alt](https://github.com/kuob10/GrandMarket-Retail-Sales-Analysis/blob/ce600cbf1149860231581a87c034f9368d7e0200/Images/sale_summary.jpg)
#### Revenue Volatility

**Patterns Observed:**
- Drastic fluctuations in revenue throughout the year
- **Peak Months:** May, August, October, and December
- **Valley Months:** March, July, and September

**Average Revenue Analysis:**
- Most months fall below the average revenue (excluding peak months)
- Peak months significantly inflate the average, making it non-representative of typical monthly performance
- Average is skewed by large spikes

#### Seasonal Trends

**Quarterly Breakdown:**

| Quarter | Trend | Details |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **Q1** | Declining | Consistent downward movement |
| **Q2** | Sharp rise | April-June, driven primarily by May |
| **Q3** | Extremely volatile | Sharp crash in July followed by peak in August |
| **Q4** | Strong overall | Peak in October, dip in November, recovery in December |

**Notable Anomalies:**
- Unusually high climb in August
- Sharp dip from October to November (unexpected given traditional holiday shopping patterns)

---

### 2. Average Order Value (AOV)

#### Volatility Pattern

- AOV fluctuates significantly from ~$160 to ~$211
- **Average AOV:** $188.11
- **Peak AOV Months:** May ($207), October ($210), December (~$210)
- **Lowest AOV Months:** March ($160), June ($172)

#### Key Insights

- May, October, and December showed spikes in both sales revenue **AND** AOV, indicating high purchasing value during these periods
- June to July shows AOV recovery to above-average levels, suggesting a volume vs. value trade-off (fewer transactions but higher customer spending)
- AOV fluctuations are moderate compared to overall sales volatility

---

### 3. Number of Orders

#### Order Volume Volatility

- Significant fluctuations ranging from 70 to 105 orders per month
- **Peak Order Months:** May (103), August (105), October (97)
- **Low Order Months:** July (70), September (71), November (72)

#### Trends by Quarter

| Quarter | Performance |
|---------|-------------|
| **Q1** | January-April below average volume with declining trend |
| **Q2** | Sharp recovery in May |
| **Q3** | Extremely volatile - sharp crash in July followed by peak in August |
| **Q4** | Peak in October, recovery in December |

#### Critical Insight

**August Anomaly:** High sales revenue and high order volume, but **LOW AOV**
- Indicates customers were purchasing smaller quantities per transaction or less premium items
- Suggests promotional/clearance activity driving volume over value

- Home category shows slightly counter-cyclical behavior

**Overall Trend:**
- Product category revenue volatility mirrors overall sales volatility

## Recommendations

### üí∞ Sales Strategy

#### 1. Address Mid-Year Revenue Decline

**Tactics:**
- Launch "Mid-Year Clearance" campaign (June-July)
- Implement flash sales throughout Q3
- Create urgency with limited-time promotions ("Don't miss out: X% off")
- Lower free shipping thresholds during valley months

#### 2. Fix November Anomaly

**Investigation Areas:**
- Marketing timing (too early or too late for holiday shopping?)
- Competitive pressure during Black Friday/Cyber Monday
- Inventory availability issues
- Customer budget exhaustion after October spending

**Recommendation:**
- Launch "Early Black Friday" promotions (November 1-15)
- Extend October momentum with seamless transition campaigns
